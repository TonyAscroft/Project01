The new Art Deco inspired design of the Aero 8 was a head turner, but the performance upgrade was astounding, 170 mph! In 2001 production began on the Aero 8, followed by the Aero GT, Aero Max and Aero SuperSports. Meanwhile Morgan continued to race and develop better mechanical systems (see the Nissan-powered Morgan prototype). Morgan also reintroduced the 3-wheeler in 2011. Of course, its not the same 3-wheeeler of old. The current model sports a 1.8L V-Twin engine with a 5 speed Mazda gearbox and a top speed of 115 mph (185 kph). It set record sales levels from the minute it launched and now an electric version is on its way. Driving as it was meant to be. In 2018 sales are up, wait times are down and the Morgan Plus 8 Anniversary Edition was sold out before production even began. Soichiro Honda once suggested that in the future there will be a half dozen car companies… and Morgan. Let’s hope he’s right about Morgan.
