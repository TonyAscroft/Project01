The end of the war and return of the work force meant production could resume and by 1946 Morgan Motors was shipping three-wheelers again, but the demand for cycle-cars was waning and in 1953 the last one left the factory. For fifteen years the Morgan 4/4 had seen numerous mechanical changes, but the overall body design and 'hand made' production methods had changed very little. The 4/4 started out as a 1100cc 35hp but when they dropped in a 2000cc 68hp Vanguard engine in 1950 they created a new model, the Plus 4. In 1954 the flat radiator front end was changed to hide the radiator behind a curved grill and the iconic Morgan design was complete. Engines and transmissions would change over the years, Vanguard, TR2, TR3, and performance would improve but the ‘look’ of the Morgan saw only minor upgrades. In 1968 Morgan once again paired their iconic design with an even larger engine, a 184hp aluminium Rover V8. This boosted the top speed to over 125 mph! The Plus 8 replaced the Plus 4.
