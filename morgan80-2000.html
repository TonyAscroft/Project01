The tradition of only upgrading engines and mechanics carried on through the 80’s and 90’s. In 1985 the Plus 4 was reintroduced with a Fiat engine (currently a 2.0 L Ford engine). All models were updated in 1997, to meet modern safety regulations and streamline production. Aluminum superform wings, longer doors, and a redesigned dash to allow for airbags was added but the style remained the same. A classic style, imitated but never improved upon. Morgan had become a boutique car company producing just three models the 4/4, the Plus 4 and the Plus 8. Concern about the longevity of Morgan began to circulate. The engines were changing, the public acknowledged the improvements but there hadn’t been a new model in over thirty years. Could a hand made car company keep going in the modern market? Selling so few cars annually? We'll never know because Morgan began designing a new chassis and body to go with the BMW 4.4-litre V8 engine and in 2001 the Aero 8 debuted.
