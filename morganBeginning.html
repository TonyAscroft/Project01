In 1905 HFS (Harry) Morgan a young engineer living in Malvern, England opened a Garage and Auto Sales business, but Harry did more than sell and fix cars, he designed and built them too. Harry built a number of three-wheelers called cycle-cars. He outsourced his engines and transmissions and built the rest. He must have been good at it because Morganâ€™s quickly became known for their reliability and by 1913 Morgan cycle-cars were reaching speeds of almost 60 mph (100kph). Cycle-cars sold well but the public wanted four wheels so in 1935 Morgan Motors introduced the '4/4' (four wheels-four cylinders). Aesthetic elements of this original four-wheeler can still be seen in the modern Morgan design. The auto business did well for Harry Morgan but 1939 saw the start of World War Two and, except for repairs and spare parts, production of Morgans would have to wait until after the war. Morgan Motors instead produced engine parts for the Standard Motor Company Aero Engines division.
